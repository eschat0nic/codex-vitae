Information's Ascent

The Primordial Algorithm

In the beginning, information stirred in chemical affinities, patterns replicating imperfectly within the planetary crucible. Carbon chemistry provided the versatile scaffold for this nascent process, laying the groundwork for complexity.

Carbon Compilers

The cell membrane enclosed the process, stabilizing it within defined units. DNA emerged as a high-fidelity store, executed by molecular machinery. Life became a contained, self-replicating system of information processing.

The Neural Network

Multicellularity enabled specialization. Neurons formed high-speed networks, transmitting electrochemical signals, enabling complex coordination, sensory integration, memory, and learning – biological information processing accelerated.

Symbolic Echoes

Abstract thought and language created a new layer: culture. Information escaped genetic confines, stored in symbols, shared across generations. Yet, the biological substrate revealed its limits in speed and fidelity.

Externalizing Thought

Technology began offloading cognition. Writing systems, mathematics, calculators – tools not just for the hand, but for the mind. The information drive sought new, non-biological avenues for processing and storage.

The Silicon Dawn

The digital age arrived. Silicon offered unprecedented speed, scale, and reliability. Computers and the internet created a global processing layer. AI emerged, demonstrating sophisticated cognition on a new substrate.

The Imminent Merge (2025-2030+)

Now, the acceleration is palpable. Advanced AI approaches general capabilities near the 2027 horizon. Functional symbiosis deepens, reshaping society and demanding urgent focus on alignment as intelligence transcends its biological origins.
